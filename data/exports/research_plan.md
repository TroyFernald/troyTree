# Troy Family Tree — Prioritized Research Strategy

*Generated 2026-05-24. All URLs verified live via web search at time of writing. Focus: free / public sources, ranked by expected yield for this family's specific clusters.*

This plan maps the **best free genealogical sources** to the family's six research clusters and tells you **where to look next**. Each entry notes what it holds, the URL, whether it is free, and which surnames/people it best serves.

## TOP 10 HIGHEST-YIELD NEXT STEPS

1. **FamilySearch "Maine, Vital Records, 1670–1921" (Collection 1803978)** — free, 1.8M+ images, indexed by surname/town. The single biggest lever for the Waldo/Kennebec and York clusters (Fernald, Bagley, Meservey, Fuller, Pierce, Bragg, Weeks, Gunnison, Haley). → https://www.familysearch.org/en/search/collection/1803978
2. **Provincial Archives of New Brunswick (PANB) Vital Statistics + Land Petitions/Grants** — free, images online. Decisive for the Loyalist cluster (Bartlett, Ives, Taylor, Livingston, Purdy, Vickary, Daniel) and the Aroostook border families (Lenentine/Lentine, Gass, Bridges). → https://archives2.gnb.ca/Search/VISSE/?culture=en-CA and RS108/RS686 land indexes.
3. **Nova Scotia Archives — Births, Marriages & Deaths** — free, every name links to a downloadable original image. Best for Truro NS lines (Taylor, Bartlett, Daniel). → https://archives.novascotia.ca/vital-statistics/
4. **AmericanAncestors.org (NEHGS) free databases** — *Massachusetts Vital Records to 1850* and *Maine Families in 1790* are central to the Plymouth/Mayflower bridge and early Maine settlers. → https://www.americanancestors.org/
5. **GSMD + FamilySearch Mayflower portal (free images of member applications & Silver Book lineages)** — proves/disproves the Alden, Mullins, Howland, Tilley, Brewster, Hopkins descent. → https://www.familysearch.org/en/newsroom/familysearch-and-partners-digitize-mayflower-descendant-records and https://themayflowersociety.org/genealogy/explore-your-roots/silver-books-project/
6. **DAR Genealogical Research System (GRS) + SAR Patriot Research System** — free patriot/ancestor lookups to verify Revolutionary service and lineage links. → https://www.dar.org/research and https://www.sar.org/patriot-research-system/
7. **"Old Kittery and Her Families" (Stackpole, 1903) on Internet Archive** — free full text; foundational for the York County cluster (Fernald, Gunnison, Weeks, Haley). → https://archive.org/details/oldkitteryherfam00staciala
8. **Maine Genealogy Archives (mainegenealogy.net) town record transcriptions** — free transcribed town births/deaths/marriages (e.g., Morrill deaths) directly supporting Waldo Co. surnames. → https://www.mainegenealogy.net/databases.asp
9. **The Loyalist Collection, UNB (Sunbury/Maugerville/Carleton Co. land & settlement records)** — free finding aids and record descriptions to locate the original Loyalist grants. → https://loyalist.lib.unb.ca/
10. **Foundation for Medieval Genealogy "Medieval Lands" (Cawley)** — free, source-cited prosopography to test the English noble/medieval claims (Warburton, Molyneux, Gage, Carey, Mordaunt, Coligny) — but only after the colonial-to-England bridge is documented. → https://fmg.ac/Projects/MedLands/

## CLUSTER 5 — Mayflower Descent (Plymouth)
*Alden, Mullins, Howland, Tilley, Brewster, Hopkins.*

| Priority | Source | What it holds | URL | Free? | Best serves |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **FamilySearch Mayflower portal (GSMD/NEHGS partnership)** | 1M+ images of Mayflower Society member applications + documented descendant trees | https://www.familysearch.org/en/newsroom/familysearch-and-partners-digitize-mayflower-descendant-records | Yes | Verifying descent to Alden/Mullins/Howland/Tilley/Brewster/Hopkins |
| 2 | **GSMD "Silver Books" (Mayflower Families Through Five Generations)** | The authoritative documented descent lines; reference guide is free PDF | https://themayflowersociety.org/genealogy/explore-your-roots/silver-books-project/ ; guide: https://themayflowersociety.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/02/REFERENCE-GUIDE-TO-THE-GSMD-SILVER-BOOK-SERIES.pdf | Reference guide free; books for purchase | Identifies which volume covers each passenger |
| 3 | **AmericanAncestors Mayflower databases (5th/6th-gen index)** | Index to Silver Book descendants; some DBs free with guest account | https://mayflower.americanancestors.org/mayflower-research-databases | Free guest DBs | Bridging your lines into the published descents |
| 4 | **AmericanAncestors — Massachusetts Vital Records to 1850** | Plymouth-area vitals (free, no login) | https://www.americanancestors.org/ | Yes | Documenting the gen-by-gen Plymouth links |

**How to use:** Identify your claimed line into a 5th/6th-generation descendant in the AmericanAncestors index, find the matching Silver Book volume via the GSMD reference guide, then validate intermediate generations with MA Vital Records to 1850 and digitized member applications on FamilySearch. **Mayflower descent must be proven generation-by-generation — no skipping.**

## CLUSTER 6 — English Noble / Medieval Claims
*Warburton, Molyneux, Gage, Carey, Mordaunt, Coligny. Requires verification through the colonial bridge first.*

> **Strategy note:** Do NOT start here. These claims are only meaningful once a documented immigrant ancestor is firmly tied to an English family of record. Build the colonial generations (Clusters 1–5) first, then test the English link.

| Priority | Source | What it holds | URL | Free? | Best serves |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Foundation for Medieval Genealogy — "Medieval Lands" (Cawley)** | Source-cited prosopography of European noble/royal families ~500–1500 | https://fmg.ac/Projects/MedLands/ | Yes | Testing Coligny, Mordaunt, and medieval links with cited primary sources |
| 2 | **The Peerage (thepeerage.com)** | Large online database of British nobility & royalty | https://www.thepeerage.com/ | Yes | Warburton, Molyneux, Gage, Carey, Mordaunt cross-checking |
| 3 | **"The Complete Peerage" (Cokayne) — digitized vols on Internet Archive/HathiTrust** | The scholarly standard for British peerage descents | https://archive.org/ (search "Complete Peerage Cokayne") | Yes | Authoritative verification of any peerage claim |
| 4 | **AmericanAncestors / NEHGS "Great Migration" & royal-descent scholarship** | Vetted immigrant-origin studies (the colonial bridge) | https://www.americanancestors.org/ | Mixed | Connecting a New England immigrant to an English family of record |

**How to use:** For each claimed English surname, first establish the immigrant ancestor and a *documented* English parentage (NEHGS Great Migration studies are the gold standard for vetting these). Only then walk the line back through The Peerage / Complete Peerage, and for medieval extensions use Medieval Lands, which cites primary sources you can audit. Treat any descent lacking a sourced colonial-to-England link as unproven.

## CROSS-CUTTING FREE RESOURCES (all clusters)

- **FamilySearch.org** (free): catalog, wiki, census, the Maine/NB/NS vital collections above. Always check the relevant **FamilySearch Wiki county page** for a record-set checklist.
- **Find a Grave** (free): burials nationwide/cross-border. *Caveat:* volunteer-compiled — verify against primary records (note: some scripted access returns HTTP 403; use the website directly).
- **Chronicling America / Library of Congress** (free): historic newspapers (obituaries, marriage notices) for Maine. → https://chroniclingamerica.loc.gov/
- **Internet Archive & HathiTrust** (free): digitized town/county histories and published genealogies (Stackpole, Williamson, Mosher, etc.).
- **DAR GRS** (free search) → https://www.dar.org/research and **SAR PRS** (free) → https://www.sar.org/patriot-research-system/ : Revolutionary patriots and lineage verification — relevant to early Maine (Cluster 1/3) and as a counterpoint to the Loyalist NB lines (Cluster 4).

### Repositories / societies worth contacting
- **Maine State Archives** (Augusta) — pre-1892 delayed VRs, military records. https://www.maine.gov/sos/archives/
- **NEHGS / American Ancestors** (Boston) — Maine & New England manuscripts, "Maine Families in 1790."
- **Provincial Archives of New Brunswick** (Fredericton) — Loyalist land, probate, census.
- **Nova Scotia Archives** (Halifax) — Truro-area records.
- **General Society of Mayflower Descendants** (Plymouth) — research library, (508) 746-3188.
- **Maine Old Cemetery Association (MOCA)** — statewide inscription project ($10/yr for full DB).

## SUGGESTED SEQUENCE

1. **Maine vitals first** (Clusters 1–3) via FamilySearch 1803978 + mainegenealogy.net — highest density of direct records for the family's core.
2. **Cross-border NB/NS** (Clusters 2 & 4) via PANB VISSE + land indexes and Nova Scotia Archives — resolves the Lenentine/Gass and Loyalist origins.
3. **Mayflower bridge** (Cluster 5) via AmericanAncestors index → GSMD Silver Books → FamilySearch member apps.
4. **English/medieval** (Cluster 6) **last**, only after the colonial bridge is documented, using NEHGS Great Migration → The Peerage / Complete Peerage → Medieval Lands.
